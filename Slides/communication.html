Communication between services

  • Some problems to take into account:
    • sender and receiver must be connected;
    • prevent high level information flows to low level services;
    • prevent high level information flows to low level nodes.
  • We opt for providing a representation of synchronous communications, which fits well the fog environment.
  • Problog provides an easy way to express the probability of communication failure.

More infrastructure

  • Nodes are connected by (bidirectional) links.
    • \(p::link(LId,\ Latency,\ [NId1, NId2])\).
  • Links are associated to a latency, according to a certain probability.
  • Two services can communicate if their hosting nodes are connected.
  • The \(findRoute\) predicate also takes care of checking that the security level of each traversed node agrees with the label of the information sent.
    • ×
    • findRoute(_, Latency, Latency, _, Source, Source, _, []).
      findRoute(AOp, OldLatency, Latency, Old, Source, Dest, ReqSecurity, [Source | Route]) :-
      Source \== Dest,
      isConnected(Source, Step, LId, LinkLatency),
      Old \== Step,
      OldLatency =< LinkLatency,
      labelL(AOp, LId, ReqSecurity),
      findRoute(AOp, LinkLatency, Latency, Source, Step, Dest, ReqSecurity, Route).
    • findRoute(AOp, OldLatency, Latency, Old, Source, Dest, ReqSecurity, [Source | Route]) :-
      Source \== Dest,
      isConnected(Source, Step, LId, LinkLatency),
      Old \== Step,
      LinkLatency =< OldLatency,
      labelL(AOp, LId, ReqSecurity),
      findRoute(AOp, OldLatency, Latency, Source, Step, Dest, ReqSecurity, Route).
    • isConnected(Source, Step, L, LinkLatency) :- link(L, LinkLatency, [Source, Step]); link(L, LinkLatency, [Step, Source]).

A new language construct

  • We provide a send construct, assuming that synchronous communication has an associated timeout.
    • \(send(Receiver, Values, Timeout)\).
  • Instead of providing an explicit receive construct, we exploit probability by providing a responseTime predicate.
    • \(p::responseTime(Receiver, Time)\).

Security context of send

  • If the receiver is reachable, we recompute its security context by taking into account the received values.
  • To do that, we also provide each service with an environment.
  • To send a value, a service must have it in its environment.
    • ×
    • ctx(AOp, send(Args, Service, Timeout), L, Env, Env, History, History) :-
      responseTime(Service, Time),
      Time =< Timeout,
      subset(Args, Env),
      labelF(AOp, Args, L),
      service(Service, _, Prog, _, _, _),
      ctx(AOp, Prog, L2, Args, ServEnv, [], ServHistory),
      leq2(AOp, L, L2).
    • ctx(AOp, send(Args, Service, Timeout), L, Env, Env, History, History) :-
      responseTime(Service, Time),
      Timeout =< Time,
      subset(Args, Env),
      labelF(AOp, Args, L).
